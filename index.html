Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about how Ballpark works

How is Ballpark different from freelance platforms?

Unlike traditional freelance platforms, Ballpark focuses on product creation rather than project work. Developers build solutions they own and can scale, while problem owners become early customers rather than one-time clients. This creates better alignment of incentives - developers are motivated to build lasting solutions, and problem owners get better pricing through SaaS models.

What kind of problems can I submit?

We focus on business problems that can be solved with software and have potential for productization. Ideal submissions are challenges that other businesses likely face too, enabling developers to build solutions that scale beyond a single customer. One-off project requests or highly customized solutions are better suited for traditional freelance platforms.

How does pricing work?

As a problem owner, you specify what you'd be willing to pay monthly for a solution. Developers use this to gauge market potential and price their products. You'll typically get early adopter pricing and influence over the product direction in exchange for being an initial customer. There's no upfront cost - you only pay once a solution is built that meets your needs.

What's in it for developers?

Developers get access to validated problems with committed budgets, eliminating the riskiest part of building products - finding customers. You own what you build and can sell to other customers facing similar challenges. Instead of trading time for money, you're building assets that can generate recurring revenue.

What if no one picks up my problem?

While we can't guarantee every problem will find a developer, we actively curate submissions and work to match problems with interested developers. We'll provide feedback on making your submission more attractive and can help refine the problem statement to increase chances of finding a match.

Can developers get early access to problems?

Yes, developers can subscribe for early access to new problems before they're publicly listed. This gives you first opportunity to connect with businesses and start building solutions. Early access members also get detailed problem briefs and direct introductions to problem owners.

How can businesses increase visibility for their problems?

Businesses can promote their problems to get more developer attention or offer bounties to accelerate development. Promoted problems are featured prominently and shared directly with our developer network. Bounties can help attract top developers and speed up the solution timeline.

Who owns the products built on Ballpark?

Developers retain 100% ownership of the products they build. While the initial solution is inspired by a specific business problem, developers have complete freedom to expand, modify, and commercialize their products. This includes all intellectual property rights and the ability to sell to additional customers beyond the original problem owner.
